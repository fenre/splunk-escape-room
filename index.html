01 The Concept

An inverse escape room where players break in instead of out. Use Splunk to crack a vault guarded by seven seals.

Christmas Eve. Nakatomi Plaza, Los Angeles. A tower of glass and steel, and tonight there's a party on the thirtieth floor. You're not here for the party. You're part of the team that just took the building. There's a vault. Seven locks. Bearer bonds inside — a fortune. The man who knew the codes isn't giving them up. So we crack it ourselves.
🔍

Splunk Is the Room

The "escape room" is a Splunk environment. Players investigate synthetic log data to discover codes, sequences, and procedures.

🏛

Physical Vault Model

A tangible Nakatomi Plaza model with 7 seal inputs. Codes from Splunk are entered on the physical vault to open each seal.

🎬

Die Hard Theme

Christmas Eve, 1988. Players are Hans Gruber's tech crew. Easter eggs, quotes, and characters from the film are woven throughout.

⚡

The Seventh Seal

Six seals open with codes. The seventh is a meta solution — a physical trick outside the system, just like in the movie.

02 Game Phases

45–60 minutes. Four phases plus the final seal. Each phase builds on the last.

0:00 – 0:08 · ~8 min
Phase 1 — Lobby (Infiltration)
Enter the building's data in Splunk. Prove you have access. Run your first search.
McClane hint: "Unaccounted for person in building"
0:08 – 0:18 · ~10 min
Phase 2 — Reconnaissance
Map the tower: floors, cameras, guards, restricted areas. Find the vault floor. Build a dashboard.
McClane hint: "Radio traffic — unknown unit"
0:18 – 0:28 · ~10 min
Phase 3 — Access Control
Find a bypass code, badge ID, or time window. Unlock the vault phase. Takagi won't help.
McClane hint: "Personnel down — we've lost contact"
0:28 – 0:50 · ~22 min
Phase 4 — The Vault (Seals 1–6)
Open six seals with codes found in Splunk. Use rex, transaction, lookups, and time ranges to extract them.
McClane hint: "Movement in HVAC" / "Just another American who saw too many movies"
0:50 – 1:00 · ~10 min
Seal 7 — The Meta Solution
No code in Splunk. The final seal requires a physical trick — unplug the power, flip a hidden switch, or cut the cable.
McClane hint: "He's here."

Seals 1–6: Data-Driven

Each seal's code is hidden in vault logs, maintenance logs, or Takagi's memos. Players extract them with SPL commands like rex, transaction, lookup, and time-range searches. Codes are entered on the physical model's keypad, dials, or switches.

Seal 7: The Movie Twist

The system never tells you how to open the last seal. Options: unplug the vault's power cable (relay detects power loss), find a hidden switch on the model, or "cut" a prop cable. The data might hint: "failsafe: secondary release on power loss".

06 Data Model

Vault Logs nakatomi_vault timestamp, action, detail, result Code attempts, door status, seal protocols
Building / Sensor nakatomi_building timestamp, system, event, floor, value Elevator, HVAC, power, camera events
Lookup — floor_id → name Floor names: "Lobby", "Vault Floor", etc.

07 Threats & Fail Conditions

Multiple ways to lose — all lead to the same ending: Hans's fate.

Police Breach

Player triggers an external alert (wrong search, trap code, or milestone tied to the movie).
"SWAT has the building. You're arrested."

McClane

3 wrong seal code attempts on the physical model.
"McClane got the drop on you."

Roof Early

Player enters a decoy/trap code during Phase 4 that triggers the roof charges.
"You triggered the charges early."

Roof Late

Timer expires (0:55–1:00) before all 7 seals are open.
"The charges blew. You never got the vault."
