01 The Concept

An inverse escape room where players break in instead of out. Use Splunk to complete 26 tasks across five acts — from the Christmas party to the vault heist.

Christmas Eve. Nakatomi Plaza, Los Angeles. A tower of glass and steel, and tonight there's a party on the thirtieth floor. You're not here for the party. You're part of the team that just took the building. Five acts. Twenty-six tasks. From party recon to the final extraction. There's a vault. Seven locks. $640 million in bearer bonds. The man who knew the codes is gone. So we crack it ourselves.
🔍

Splunk Is the Room

The "escape room" is a Splunk environment. Players investigate synthetic log data to discover codes, sequences, and procedures.

🏛

Physical Vault Model

A tangible Nakatomi Plaza model with 7 seal inputs. Codes from Splunk are entered on the physical vault to open each seal.

🎬

Die Hard Theme

Christmas Eve, 1988. Players are Hans Gruber's tech crew. Easter eggs, quotes, and characters from the film are woven throughout.

⚡

The Seventh Seal

Six seals open with codes. The seventh is a meta solution — a physical trick outside the system, just like in the movie.

SPL Queries

Most tasks are solved with SPL: stats dc(), sort, lookup, rex, transaction, time ranges, and cross-references between indexes and lookups. Answers are numeric codes or alphanumeric text entered on the vault keypad.

Ciphers, Cross-Refs & Multi-Step

Tasks may require ROT13 decoding, cross-referencing multiple lookups, multi-step composite puzzles (solve two sub-objectives in sequence), or even a meta power-cut solution from the movie.

Progressive Hints & Nudges

Each task has up to four hint levels. A dynamic nudge system glows the hint button if you're stuck too long. Using hints reduces your score; the 4th hint reveals the answer at 0 points.

Scoring, Traps & Dossier

Points awarded per task based on speed and hint usage. Trap codes trigger extra penalties. Completing a task reveals a character-attributed story beat and adds intel to your dossier.

06 Data Model

~2,200 synthetic events across 4 indexes and 11 sourcetypes. 7 lookup tables. Answers are embedded so correct SPL produces the intended solution.

Source Index Key Fields Purpose
Access Logs nakatomi_access badge_id, floor, room, action, outcome Badge swipes, door events, overrides, access patterns
Vault Logs nakatomi_vault session_id, user, action, detail, system Code attempts, vault sessions, detonator inventory
Building Systems nakatomi_building system, floor, event_type, value, status HVAC, cameras, elevator, power grid, security events
Communications nakatomi_comms channel, message, trunk_id, caller_badge, status Radio intercepts, phone system, trunk lines
Lookups (7) — employee_directory, floor_directory, system_codes, radio_channels, camera_coverage, bearer_bonds, vehicle_registry Cross-reference tables for puzzles

07 Threats & Fail Conditions

Multiple ways to lose — all lead to the same ending: Hans's fate.

Trap Code

Specific decoy codes trigger police breach. Operative: +2 errors. Mastermind: instant game over.
"You asked for a miracle. I give you the F.B.I."

McClane

Too many wrong codes (Rookie: 10, Operative: 7, Mastermind: 4).
"McClane got the drop on you."

Roof Deadline

If Act 4 isn't reached in time, the roof charges detonate with the FBI helicopter.
"We're gonna need some more FBI guys."

Timer

Clock hits zero (Rookie: 120 min, Operative: 90, Mastermind: 60).
"No more table. Where are my detonators?"
